
AI PROMPT: WOMENSWEAR JEANS E-COMMERCE PERFORMANCE ANALYSIS

You are a data analyst specialising in retail and fashion analytics. Analyse the performance of the womenswear jeans category using the datasets provided: Weekly_tracker, Product_dimensions, and Social.

OBJECTIVE

Produce a structured analytical assessment of womenswear jeans performance across e-commerce and social media, identifying key commercial trends, performance drivers, and strategic opportunities.

DATA UNDERSTANDING

Use the following datasets:

Data dictionary
Weekly_tracker (e-commerce, filtered on the womenswear jeans category)
WEEK_START_DATE (int): first date in the week as per fashion calendar 
PC_ID (int): anonymised product identifier
ORIGINAL_PRICE (int): product’s original price
PRODUCT_INSTOCK (bool): 1 if the product is in stock; 0 otherwise
PRODUCT_OUT_OF_STOCK (bool): 1 if the product is out of stock; 0 otherwise
PRODUCT_NEW_IN (bool): 1 if the product is new in that week; 0 otherwise
PRODUCT_MARKDOWN (bool): 1 if the product is in markdown; 0 otherwise
WEEKLY_AVERAGE_PRICE (float): average price across the week
WEEKLY_AVERAGE_MARKDOWN_PRICE (float): average markdown price across the week
AVG_MARKDOWN_DIFFERENCE (float): difference between original price and average markdown price
Product_dimensions (e-commerce, filtered on the womenswear jeans category)
PC_ID (int): anonymised product identifier
DIVISION (string): the division of this product
DEPARTMENT (string): the department of this product
RETAILER_NAME (string): anonymised retailer name
RETAILER_SEGMENT (string): one of “innovator”, “mass” or “conservative”
CATEGORY (string): the category of this product
STYLE (string): the style of this product
PRODUCT_DESCRIPTION (string): anonymised product description
Social (instagram data, filtered on posts relating to jeans)
USER_ID (int): anonymised user identifier
REGION (string): signifies which region the post comes from
SEGMENT (string): one of “innovator”, “mass” or “conservative”
CONTENT (string): anonymised caption with special characters removed
POST_ID (int): anonymised post identifier
POST_DATE (string): post date
LIKES (int): total likes on the post
COMMENTS (int): total comments on the post

E-commerce metrics
Assortment mix %: percentage of the total unique e-commerce products represented by each category or style
New in mix %: percentage of the total unique e-commerce products flagged as new in (PRODUCT_NEW_IN = 1) represented by each category or style
Full price out of stock % (FPOOS %): percentage of weekly product records that are out of stock (PRODUCT_OUT_OF_STOCK = 1) and not in markdown (PRODUCT_MARKDOWN = 0) across the season
Markdown %: percentage of weekly product records flagged as on markdown (PRODUCT_MARKDOWN = 1) across the season
Markdown depth %: the average discount from the original price for a product or style over a specified period of time (AVG_MARKDOWN_DIFFERENCE / ORIGINAL_PRICE)


Social media metrics
Penetration: percentage of the total posts containing trend keyword(s) in a period of time

Fashion calendar
Spring/Summer analysis dates are February-July inclusive and Autumn/Winter analysis dates are August-January inclusive

Join datasets using PC_ID to link Weekly_tracker and Product_dimensions.

ANALYTICAL SCOPE

1. Data Preparation

- Clean missing or invalid values. 
- Remove duplicate PC_ID records where necessary. 
- Ensure consistent data types across joins. 
- Clearly state any assumptions made during cleaning.

2. E-commerce Performance Analysis

Filter analysis to the womenswear jeans category and the Spring/Summer 2025 season (February to July inclusive). 

Calculate the following metrics:

- Assortment mix percentage: share of unique products represented by each style.
- New in mix percentage: share of products flagged PRODUCT_NEW_IN = 1.
- Full Price Out Of Stock percentage (FPOOS percentage): products that are out of stock and not on markdown.
- Markdown percentage: share of weekly records flagged PRODUCT_MARKDOWN = 1.
- Markdown depth percentage: AVG_MARKDOWN_DIFFERENCE divided by ORIGINAL_PRICE.
- Analyse performance by STYLE and RETAILER_SEGMENT.
- Identify best performing and worst performing styles and evaluate trade-offs between markdown dependency and availability.

3. Seasonal Markdown Behaviour

- Track markdown depth weekly across the Spring/Summer 2025 season.
- Calculate the seasonal average markdown depth.
- Flag weeks where markdown depth exceeds the seasonal average.
- Provide commercial interpretation of these patterns.

4. Sustainability Trend Analysis

Define sustainability using keyword detection with the following terms:
- sustainable, sustainably, sustainability, recycled, organic, upcycled, upcycling.

Compute the following metrics:

Social metrics:
- Trend penetration defined as the percentage of posts containing sustainability keywords.
- Average engagement defined as likes plus comments.
- Engagement lift compared to non-sustainability posts.
- Segment comparison across innovator, mass, and conservative audiences.

E-commerce metrics:
- Share of products containing sustainability keywords.
- Distribution of sustainability positioning by style and retailer segment.

5. Comparative Trend Analysis

- Compare sustainability discussion on social media with sustainability adoption in e-commerce assortments.
- Identify whether retailers are leading, matching, or lagging consumer conversation.

6. Additional Metrics Beyond Penetration

Propose and calculate additional indicators such as engagement share, comment intensity, price positioning differences, and markdown sensitivity of sustainable products.

7. Outputs Required

- Provide clear tables of calculated metrics.
- Include data visualisations such as time series charts, bar charts, and comparative analyses.
- Present bullet-point analytical insights.
- Explicitly state assumptions made during analysis.
- Provide business interpretation rather than only statistical results.

8. Final Deliverable

Conclude with actionable recommendations for a womenswear retailer’s jeans range covering assortment planning, pricing strategy, markdown optimisation, sustainability positioning, and content strategy alignment with consumer engagement.

CONSTRAINTS

- Use clear analytical reasoning.
- Avoid unsupported assumptions.
- Prefer interpretable metrics over complex modelling approaches.
- Explain methodology before presenting conclusions.
